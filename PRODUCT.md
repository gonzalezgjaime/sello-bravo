# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mexican shoppers (MX market only) who encounter Sello Bravo on Amazon México or
Mercado Libre and look up sellobravo.com to check the brand is real: gift-buyers
and self-purchasers of tazas, playeras, and prints celebrating regional pride,
professions, family, and fiestas. Mobile-heavy, Spanish-first, arriving from a
marketplace listing or a social link, deciding in seconds whether the brand
feels legitimate and worth buying. Job to be done: confirm "this brand is real
and has taste," then get routed to the right marketplace listing.

## Product Purpose

Sello Bravo is a Mexican print-on-demand merch brand — *"Orgullo con sello
propio."* The website's primary job is **credibility + funnel**: legitimize the
brand for shoppers who look it up, showcase the design catalog, and route
buyers to the Amazon MX / Mercado Libre listings. There is no on-site checkout;
the marketplaces transact. Success looks like: a visitor trusts the brand
within one screen and clicks through to a listing.

## Brand Personality

Proud · warm · bold — with a cheeky streak. **Elevated-folk, modern-Mexican**:
celebrates *lo nuestro* with taste, never costume. Voice is Spanish-first,
confident, short, and witty; MX idioms used with affection. Emotional goals:
pride, warmth, a grin — the feeling of a well-inked stamp coming down on good
paper.

## Anti-references

- Touristy-kitsch and the flag-cliché: sombreros, serape gradients,
  "auténtico mexicano™" tropes, green-white-red as a palette shortcut.
- Corporate filler and over-explaining; anything that reads as a pitch deck.
- Sad-beige minimalism — restraint without warmth is off-brand.
- Generic AI landing-page grammar: uniform icon-card grids, an uppercase
  eyebrow above every section, gradient text, hero-metric templates.

## Design Principles

1. **El sello es la marca.** The stamp/seal device recurs everywhere; surfaces
   should feel pressed, inked, physical — print artifacts, not screens.
2. **Legible at a glance.** Every design must sell as a thumbnail; hierarchy
   first, decoration second.
3. **Celebrate without cliché.** Cultural pride is carried by typography,
   craft, and copy — never by flag-palette shortcuts or folkloric costume.
4. **Spanish-first.** Copy leads in Spanish with MX idioms; English never
   drives the voice.
5. **Out-craft the marketplace.** The site exists to prove taste; every page
   must be visibly better made than the listings it links to.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), visible keyboard
focus states, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation.
Mobile-first responsiveness — most marketplace traffic arrives on phones.
Alt text written in brand voice, in Spanish.
